Fun Facts!

Some interesting facts about Seattle.

Fact Description
1 Seattle is known as the Emerald City due to its lush green surroundings and abundant green spaces.
2 The Space Needle, an iconic Seattle landmark, was built for the 1962 World's Fair and stands at 605 feet tall.
3 Seattle is home to Starbucks, the world-famous coffee chain, which opened its first store in Pike Place Market in 1971.
4 The city experiences a lot of rain, but it doesn't rank as one of the rainiest cities in the United States. It's known for its drizzle rather than heavy rain.
5 Pike Place Market is one of the oldest continuously operated public farmers' markets in the U.S. and is famous for its fish-throwing fishmongers.
6 The Museum of Pop Culture (MoPOP) in Seattle was designed by architect Frank Gehry and is a visually stunning building dedicated to popular culture.
7 Seattle is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, including the Puget Sound, the Olympic Mountains, and the Cascade Range.

Places To See

Some fun places in the Seattle area to check out!

Twice Sold Tales

Twice Sold Tales is a bookstore with 4 live-in cats and a sprawling inventory of used, collectible and rare titles. They are located in heart of the Capitol Hill neighborhood, a block from the Capitol Hill Link Light Rail station. Their book inventory is constantly changing, so please stop in to see what you’ll find. All of their inventory is used, sourced from various places (including customers such as yourself). They carry a large, ever-changing selection of plays, poetry, literature, sci-fi, horror, gardening, children's books, rare/collector's editions, etc.

The Seattle Aquarium

The Seattle Aquarium is the center of hands-on marine experience and preservation education in the Pacific Northwest, the Seattle Aquarium offers fun, exciting ways to discover more about the Puget Sound

It's a public aquarium is located in Seattle on Pier 59 on the Elliott Bay waterfront.
